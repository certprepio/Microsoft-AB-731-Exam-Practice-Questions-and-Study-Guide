### Question 1: What is “Red Teaming” in AI model evaluation?

A. Designing AI branding strategies  
B. Testing AI systems by attempting to exploit vulnerabilities  
C. Combining development teams  
D. Deploying AI for public testing  

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Red Teaming involves intentionally probing AI systems to uncover risks, biases, and security weaknesses before deployment.

---

### Question 2: When is the “Buy/Lease” AI strategy preferred over “Build”?

A. When the company has unlimited resources  
B. When a quick, scalable solution is needed without deep AI expertise  
C. When data is completely unique  
D. When competing with AI research companies  

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Pre-built AI solutions are faster, cost-effective, and ideal for organizations lacking advanced AI infrastructure.

---

### Question 3: Which role ensures data quality and compliance before AI deployment?

A. End User  
B. Data Steward / Governance Lead  
C. Designer  
D. Prompt Engineer  

**Answer:** B  
**Explanation:** Data governance professionals ensure secure, compliant, and high-quality data for AI systems.

---

### Question 4: What happens when a user without permission asks Copilot to summarize a protected document?

A. Copilot summarizes with a warning  
B. Copilot overrides permissions  
C. Copilot cannot access the content  
D. Copilot requests admin approval  

**Answer:** C  
**Explanation:** Microsoft Copilot respects security policies and cannot access restricted data without proper permissions.
